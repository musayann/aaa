What is this project about?

This project auctions artworks from photographers, musicians, painters and other artists as NFTs and donates the contributions to organisations that support Afghans. For more questions visit our FAQs.

Frequently asked questions

Step 1: Create an ethereum wallet
Set up your wallet via one of the following platforms:

  • Desktop: MetaMask. For additional explanation on how to use MetaMask, please watch this video.
  • iOS: Rainbow Wallet.
  • Android: Trust Wallet.

Step 2: Purchase crypto on a centralised exchange
Here are some exchanges that you can sign up an account with and use:

  • Binance
  • Kraken
  • crypto.com

Remember to connect your ethereum wallet to the centralised exchange account during the sign up process. Purchase crypto and transfer the funds from your exchange account to your wallet.

Step 3: Making your transaction
You’re almost done! Now it’s time to bid on some artwork:

  • Connect your wallet to the auction platform
  • Click on the Zora link of the artwork you wish to bid on
  • Add your bid amount and click on ‘Place Offer’

A non-fungible token (NFT) is a digital asset that is one-of-a-kind and offers proof of ownership tracked on the blockchain. Things that can be digitally represented, like pictures, music, videos and more can be turned into an NFT. To learn more, have a look at A beginner’s guide to NFTs by Linda Xie which is a great, easy-to-digest article on NFTs.

We are aware of the environmental impact of NFTs and support the work of communities who are rallying together to counter their energy consumption and carbon emission costs. Artist can choose to host their auctions on platforms like Zora, who are actively offsetting their carbon footprint. Read more about it here.

The organisations that the collected funds are dontated to, are chose by the artists. Here is a list of the organisations that can be currently supported.

Care.org
For 75 years, CARE has led the way to a better life for the world’s most vulnerable people. In 2020, CARE worked in over 100 countries, reaching more than 90 million people through 1,300 projects.

Civil Society and Human Rights Network
The Civil Society and Human Rights network consists of 58 Afghan organizations which are active in the promotion of human rights. The member organizations have so far all their main offices in Kabul but have representatives also in other regions of Afghanistan. Besides its member organizations, CSHRN works together with over one hundred partner organizations in different regions of Afghanistan. Read an external review of the network here.
